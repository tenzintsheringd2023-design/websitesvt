About Me

Hi! My name is Tenzin Tshering Drukar. I am from Trashi Yangtse, the eastern part of Bhutan.

1. Origami

One skill or strength I am proud of is my interest in learning origami. The first thing I learnt to make was a crane from my friend. Before, I didn't even know how to make a simple aeroplane. But after I learnt how to make a crane from my friend, I realized how fun and interesting it was. Moreover, it also taught me how to be patient, as I realized one single mistake in folding could ruin the whole output I wanted. Now I have learnt lots of things such as making flowers (important if you want to gift someone a bouquet), butterflies, dragons, frogs, cats, etc., and cranes (obviously). I mostly learn it from online tutorials.

Origami is the traditional Japanese art of folding paper into shapes such as animals, flowers, and objects. It is more than just a creative hobby—it helps develop focus, patience, and problem-solving skills. It also improves hand–eye coordination and fine motor skills. In schools, origami is used to teach mathematics, including geometry, symmetry, and fractions. Beyond creativity, it reduces stress, encourages relaxation, and allows people to express themselves through art. Origami is important because it connects culture, creativity, and learning in a simple yet meaningful way.

3. Emotional Support

One of my strengths is my ability to give emotional support to the people around me. I make sure others are heard, valued, and understood, especially during difficult or stressful moments. I create a safe space where people can talk about their thoughts without feeling judged. By listening with care and offering encouragement, I help others feel less alone and more confident in themselves. Emotional support is not just about comforting someone; it is about showing them that they do not have to face their challenges by themselves, and that genuine kindness and connection can make a real difference.
